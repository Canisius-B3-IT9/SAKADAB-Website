SIP Background
Compost, a mixture of organic materials decayed to use for plant use, balances soil density, which helps in the plant's growth.
Compost aids in liquifing too-tight soils, but too loose compost aids in clumping the soil together.
This balancing enables plants to build stronger roots in the soil, which supports stronger development.
The value of compost to plants is that it can help the plants to grow faster, healthier, and stronger.

Problem and Hypothesis
Problem:
How will the eggshells affect the development of the Abelmoschus esculentus with compost?
Hypothesis:
If the plant is given eggshells as fertilizer, then the growth rate of the
Abelmoschus Esculentus will be faster, resulting to a bigger and fresher product.

Experiment Variables
Independent Variable: Amount of fertilizer (eggshells)
Dependent Variable: Growth Rate/Internal Structure/External Appearance of Abelmoschus esculentus (Okra)
Controlled Variables:
> Number of seeds/seedlings of Okra
> Soil Type and Quantity
> Type and Size of Pot
> Quantity of Water
> Sunlight Intensity
> Surrounding Temperature
> Location of Experiment

Materials and Equipment
In making compost, we should have organic materials that will be broken down to smaller particles,
also known as COMPOSTABLES. We require banana peels, vegetable peelings, dried leaves, eggshells,
and grass cuttings as the compost starting materials.
And for the rest of the experiment, in terms of planting, we need the Okra seeds, soil, pots,
water, and shovels (preferably those suitable for gardening such as round-digger and hand-held shovel).
